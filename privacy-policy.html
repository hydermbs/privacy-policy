Privacy Policy

Vitalsy (Android package: com.vitalsyapp.app)
Effective date: April 15, 2026
Last updated: April 15, 2026

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how information is handled when you use Vitalsy (“the App”). The App helps you track lab results over time, view trends, set health-related goals, and compare readings to prior tests and reference ranges. By using the App, you agree to this policy.

Vitalsy is a personal wellness tool. It does not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

2. Information the App processes

Depending on how you use the App, the following categories may apply:

  • Health and medical-related information you enter, such as lab values, test names, dates, notes, and goals you save inside the App.
  • Account or session identifiers used only to separate your data from other users on the same device or, if cloud features are enabled, to associate data with your account.
  • App preferences, such as whether you have completed onboarding, stored locally on your device.
  • Authentication data if you sign in with email and password when cloud sync is enabled (processed by Google Firebase Authentication as described below).

The App is not designed to collect precise location, contacts, photos, or microphone data for its core features. We do not use the App to show third-party advertising.

3. Where your data is stored

Current release (on-device): In the configuration shipped with this policy, your health records and goals are stored on your device (for example in a local database). They are not transmitted to our servers for storage unless and until a cloud sync feature is enabled in a future update.

Optional cloud sync (when enabled): If a future version enables cloud backup or sync, health data and goals may be stored in Google Firebase services (Firestore and Firebase Authentication) under your user account. When that mode is active, data is processed according to Google’s terms and privacy documentation for those products.

4. How we use information

  • To provide App features: saving, displaying, and analyzing your entries on your device.
  • To maintain your session and preferences on the device.
  • If cloud sync is enabled: to synchronize your data across devices and authenticate you.

We do not sell your personal information.

5. Third-party services

When Firebase is initialized (for example for authentication persistence or future cloud sync), Google LLC may process limited technical and account-related data as described in Google’s privacy policy and the Firebase documentation. You can review: Firebase & privacy.

The App is built with Expo and React Native; those platforms may collect standard diagnostic or operational data according to their respective policies when you build or run the software.

6. Security

We use reasonable measures appropriate to a mobile health-tracking app, including storing data locally on your device and, when cloud features are used, relying on industry-standard authentication and encrypted transport where provided by the platform and Firebase.

No method of electronic storage is 100% secure. You should protect your device with a screen lock and keep your operating system updated.

7. Retention and deletion

Data stored only on your device remains until you delete it in the App or uninstall the App, unless your device or operating system retains backups separately.

If cloud sync is enabled, data may persist in Firebase until you delete it through the App (where supported) or delete your account, subject to Firebase and Google retention practices.

8. Children

The App is not directed at children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your region). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided information, please contact us so we can delete it.

9. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or export personal data, or to object to certain processing. To exercise these rights, contact us using the email below. You can also delete local data by removing entries in the App or uninstalling the App.

10. International transfers

If you use Firebase cloud services, your information may be processed on servers located in various countries, including outside your country of residence, in accordance with Google’s practices.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top will change when we do. Continued use of the App after changes means you accept the updated policy.
